# Tweet — P07 (Graffiti 154 & 143 refuted)

**Main tweet:**

Graffiti conjecture 154 is false. It was generated by Fajtlowicz's conjecture-making program in 1986 and stayed open for ~40 years — it even survived a 2025 AI refutation paper that threw 8 search algorithms at it.

The counterexample: glue a 50-clique to a 70-edge path. Done.

**Thread reply 1:**

The conjecture says a graph's eigenvalue spread is at most n / (its average distance). A clique makes the spread big; a long tail makes distances big. Balance them and the bound breaks at n=120 — then fails harder forever (ratio grows like √n).

Bonus: sibling conjecture 143 falls to the same family at n=39 — *inside* the range the 2025 paper's search already covered. Their MCTS builds graphs edge-by-edge and simply couldn't reach dumbbell shapes. Verification is pure integer arithmetic: [repo link]
