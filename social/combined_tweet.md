Proud to say that Devin has cracked three more unsolved problems today

1. Graffiti Conjecture 154 (1988) — refuted
2. Graffiti Conjectures 39 & 40 (1988) — proved
3. Brandt's Regular Supergraph Problem (West's open-problem list) — refuted

All Lean-verified proofs & adversarially checked in the repo below

1. Graffiti Conjecture 154

Generated by Fajtlowicz's Graffiti program — one of the first "AI does math" experiments — and open for ~40 years. It even survived a 2025 paper that attacked it with 8 search algorithms up to n=50. Devin found the counterexample: glue a 50-clique to a 70-edge path (n=120). The violation reduces to a single integer inequality, proven in Lean down to the graph's distance sum.

2. Graffiti Conjectures 39 & 40

Same program, same era: the standard deviation of a graph's distance matrix never exceeds its number of positive (39) or negative (40) adjacency eigenvalues. Turns out both are TRUE — via a 4-line proof chaining Popoviciu's inequality with a 2020 spectral lemma (Geng–Wu–Wang) nobody had connected to them. Fully formalized in Lean 4 + mathlib, axioms audited.

3. Brandt's Regular Supergraph Problem

As recorded on Douglas West's open-problems list: every maximal triangle-free graph with min degree ≥ n/3 should have a regular supergraph via vertex multiplications. False — Devin found a 9-vertex counterexample where no amount of vertex cloning can ever make it regular, certified by an exact Farkas infeasibility certificate and re-proven in Lean. (Brandt's original strict > n/3 version is a known theorem; it's the recorded boundary version that breaks.)
